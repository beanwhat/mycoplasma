# Inclusion and Exclusion Principles

The project uses conservative inclusion rules so that benchmark datasets remain
traceable and reviewable.

## Inclusion Principles

- Include papers and datasets with clear organism identification and source
  provenance.
- Prefer isolate-level MIC and genotype data measured on the same isolate set.
- Record antimicrobial name, MIC method, MIC units, and genetic marker detection
  method whenever available.
- Retain enough citation and extraction metadata to audit each curated row.
- Keep background literature separate from benchmark-ready evidence when it does
  not provide same-isolate genotype-phenotype data.

## Exclusion Principles

- Exclude records with unclear organism identity or insufficient source
  provenance from curated benchmark tables.
- Do not invent MIC values, markers, isolate metadata, or phenotype categories.
- Do not convert MIC values to resistant or susceptible categories without a
  prespecified drug- and species-relevant rule.
- Do not promote aggregate summaries, review statements, or transferred marker
  rules into validated benchmark records without supporting isolate-level data.
- Do not treat genome-only or phenotype-only studies as complete
  genotype-phenotype benchmark evidence.
